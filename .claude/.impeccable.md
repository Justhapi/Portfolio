## Design Context

### Users
**Primary audience**: Recruiters and hiring managers skimming under time pressure, comparing multiple candidates. They need to feel Kathleen's craft and process within seconds — the site must earn attention fast, then reward deeper exploration.

**Secondary**: Design peers and collaborators who will judge aesthetic sensibility and systems thinking.

### Brand Personality
**Three words**: Personal · Artistic · Alive

Kathleen is a digital artist turned UX designer — someone who didn't abandon her artistic instinct when she entered systems thinking. The portfolio should feel like a person who genuinely loves making things: warm, expressive, full of small surprises, but never at the expense of clarity or professionalism.

The handmade scribble elements (Caveat font, SVG sketches, character GIF) are the right instinct. Lean into them deliberately, not decoratively.

### Aesthetic Direction
**Visual tone**: Personal-editorial. Not a case study dump, not a lookbook — somewhere between a sketchbook and a design magazine. Light base with dark hero/footer panels creates theatrical contrast. The yellow accent is bold and ownable.

**Motion**: Motion is personality here — the page should feel alive. Parallax, character animation, staggered reveals. Motion reinforces the "artist" half of the identity.

**References**: The char.gif and pixel sprite signal Kathleen's game/illustration background — lean into that nostalgia-meets-craft tension. Think: what if a Studio Ghibli background artist learned Figma? https://www.arjun-r.com https://emmiwu.com and https://jackiezhang.co.za
**Anti-references**:
- Generic Figma portfolio templates (beige grids, numbered case studies, identical cards)
- Corporate UX case study sites (all process, no personality)
- Dark mode developer portfolios (neon glows, terminal vibes)
- Anything static, predictable, or forgettable
- AI-generated aesthetic tells: gradient text, side-stripe cards, glassmorphism everywhere

### Design Principles

1. **Every interaction should feel like a discovery** — the workspace, character, icon windows, gallery behind the screen. The site rewards curiosity.

2. **Personal touches are non-negotiable** — handwriting font (Caveat), scribble decorations, pixel character. These are what make a recruiter remember this portfolio among 50 tabs.

3. **Art and systems in tension, not opposition** — the dark/yellow hero split visualizes the "artist turned designer" narrative. The structure is deliberate; the warmth is genuine.

4. **Motion earns its presence** — parallax, page-load reveals, and character animation are all narrative. Nothing moves just to move.

5. **Recruiters get the signal fast** — project titles lead with outcomes ("I built...", "I designed..."), not project names. Name + role + proof of craft visible above the fold.

### Technical Constraints
- Pure HTML/CSS/JS (no build system, no framework)
- Must work across all browser sizes (responsive)
- Pixel character sprite (64×64) requires `image-rendering: pixelated`
- Fonts: DM Serif Display (display), DM Sans (body), Caveat (handwriting accent), Fredoka One (chip labels)
- Existing palette: Cream `#FDFBF7`, Lilac `#9B8EC4`, Yellow `#F2C84A`, Dark `#0D0B0F`
